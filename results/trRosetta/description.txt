trRosetta Analysis

trRosetta is a deep learning-based protein structure prediction tool used to model the three-dimensional (3D) structure of proteins from their amino acid sequences. It predicts inter-residue distances and orientations, enabling accurate reconstruction of protein folding and spatial conformation.

In this study, trRosetta was used to generate the 3D structure of the SARS-CoV-2 membrane glycoprotein. This structural insight is important for understanding the overall organization of the protein and provides a framework for visualizing epitope locations.

The predicted structure helps validate whether selected epitopes are positioned in accessible and structurally stable regions of the protein. By integrating structural information, the analysis is refined beyond sequence-based predictions, ensuring that selected regions are not only antigenic but also structurally feasible for immune recognition.

Thus, trRosetta serves as a supporting structural validation step, enhancing the reliability of epitope selection and contributing to a more comprehensive immunoinformatics-based vaccine design pipeline.
