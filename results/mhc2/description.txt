## MHC-II Epitope Prediction and Analysis

MHC-II epitope prediction was carried out to identify helper T lymphocyte (HTL) epitopes from the SARS-CoV-2 membrane glycoprotein. These epitopes are responsible for activating CD4+ T cells and supporting the adaptive immune response.

### Process

The protein sequence was analyzed using an MHC-II binding prediction tool. The output included predicted epitopes along with IC50 values, percentile rank, antigenicity, allergenicity, and toxicity information.

### Selection Criteria

The following filters were applied to identify suitable epitopes:

* Low IC50 value
* Low percentile rank
* Antigenicity: ANTIGEN
* Allergenicity: NON-ALLERGEN
* Toxicity: NON-TOXIN

### Observations

Although several epitopes showed acceptable binding affinity, many were predicted to be allergenic or non-antigenic. This significantly reduced the number of suitable candidates.

### Purpose

The purpose of this step is to identify helper T-cell epitopes that enhance immune response coordination. However, only biologically safe and immunogenic epitopes are considered for further analysis.

### Conclusion

Due to the lack of epitopes meeting all selection criteria, MHC-II epitopes were not prioritized for downstream molecular docking studies in this project.
