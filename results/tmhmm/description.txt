TMHMM Analysis

TMHMM (TransMembrane Helix prediction using Hidden Markov Models) was used to identify transmembrane regions and predict the topology of the SARS-CoV-2 membrane glycoprotein.

This analysis helps distinguish between membrane-spanning and surface-exposed regions of the protein. In vaccine design, only extracellular or surface-accessible regions are suitable for epitope selection, as they can be recognized by the host immune system.

The TMHMM results confirmed the presence of transmembrane segments and were used to ensure that selected epitopes are located in accessible, non-transmembrane regions. The analysis is further refined by filtering out undesired hydrophobic membrane-embedded regions and focusing exclusively on fragments that are biologically safe and effective for triggering an immune response.

This step improves the reliability of epitope selection by validating their biological accessibility.
