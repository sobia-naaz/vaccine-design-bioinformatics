## Epitope Selection Strategy and Docking Justification

### Selection of MHC-I over MHC-II Epitopes

In this study, both MHC-I and MHC-II epitopes were predicted from the SARS-CoV-2 membrane glycoprotein. However, after applying stringent filtering criteria (antigenicity, allergenicity, toxicity, IC50, and rank), MHC-II epitopes were not found to be suitable for downstream analysis.

Most of the predicted MHC-II epitopes were either:

* Allergenic
* Non-antigenic
* Or did not meet binding affinity thresholds

Due to the lack of biologically safe and immunogenic candidates, MHC-II epitopes were excluded from further structural and docking analysis.

In contrast, MHC-I epitopes showed strong binding affinity, were non-allergenic, non-toxic, and antigenic. Therefore, MHC-I epitopes were selected as reliable candidates for vaccine design and further computational analysis.

---

### Selection of Top 2 Epitopes

From the filtered MHC-I dataset, two epitopes were selected:

* **ATSRTLSYYK**
* **FASYRSYRI**

These epitopes were chosen based on:

* Lowest IC50 values (strong binding affinity)
* Favorable percentile rank
* Antigenicity (ANTIGEN)
* Non-allergenicity
* Non-toxicity

Selecting the top two epitopes allows focused and efficient downstream analysis, reduces computational complexity, and highlights the most promising vaccine candidates.

---

### Why Only Two (2) Epitopes?

Instead of selecting all predicted epitopes, only the top candidates were chosen because:

* Not all predicted epitopes are biologically relevant
* Including low-quality epitopes reduces prediction accuracy
* Docking multiple peptides increases noise and complexity

Thus, selecting a small number of high-confidence epitopes ensures precision and reliability in molecular docking and vaccine design.

---

### Selection of 2A0Z for Molecular Docking

The PDB structure **2A0Z** was selected as the docking receptor because it represents a well-characterized human MHC class I molecule (HLA-A*02:01), which is widely used in immunoinformatics studies.

Reasons for selecting 2A0Z:

* High-resolution crystal structure
* Represents a common and well-studied HLA allele
* Frequently used as a standard model for peptide-MHC docking
* Structurally stable and suitable for computational analysis

Even though HLA-A*02:01 may not explicitly appear in the predicted allele list, it is commonly used as a reference structure due to its availability and reliability in docking studies.

---

### Purpose of Docking

The selected MHC-I epitopes were docked with the 2A0Z structure to:

* Evaluate binding interactions
* Analyze peptide-MHC stability
* Validate immunogenic potential at the structural level

This step provides structural confirmation that the selected epitopes can effectively bind to MHC molecules and trigger an immune response.

---

### Conclusion

The study prioritizes MHC-I epitopes due to their strong immunological relevance and reliable prediction results. By selecting high-confidence epitopes and a validated MHC structure (2A0Z), the analysis ensures accuracy, biological relevance, and robustness in the vaccine design pipeline.
