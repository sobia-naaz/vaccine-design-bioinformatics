**🧬 ChouFasman_Vaccine — Secondary Structure Analysis for Vaccine Design**
This project focuses on the application of the Chou–Fasman secondary structure prediction method to analyze protein sequences for vaccine design purposes. The tool predicts structural elements such as α-helices, β-sheets, and coil/turn regions based on amino acid propensities.
In this study, the predicted secondary structure was utilized to identify potential B-cell epitope regions, with particular emphasis on coil and turn regions, as these are typically surface-exposed and immunologically accessible. The analysis helps in selecting structurally favorable regions for designing stable and effective vaccine candidates.

**🎯 Objectives**
Predict protein secondary structure using Chou–Fasman method
Identify structurally flexible regions (coil/turns)
Support epitope selection for vaccine design
Integrate structural insights into immunoinformatics workflow

**⚙️ Methodology**
Input protein sequence in FASTA format
Perform secondary structure prediction using Chou–Fasman server
Analyze distribution of:
α-helices (H)
β-sheets (E)
turns (T)
coils (C)
Identify epitope-prone regions (coil/turns)
Interpret results in the context of vaccine design

**🧠 Key Insight**
Coil and turn regions were prioritized for epitope selection due to their flexibility and surface accessibility, making them ideal targets for immune recognition.

**🚀 Applications**
Reverse vaccinology
Epitope-based vaccine design
Structural bioinformatics analysis
Immunoinformatics pipelines

**📌 Conclusion**
The Chou–Fasman method provides valuable preliminary insights into protein structure, enabling the identification of immunologically relevant regions that can be used in designing safe, stable, and effective vaccines.
